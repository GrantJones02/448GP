Turn Order and Taking Pieces

Chess is a game played in turns,
where the player with the white pieces
moves before the player with the black pieces.

Each kind of piece has a set movement pattern
it can move relative to where it is now.

Pieces of your own color block other pieces
of your color from being moved.

Your pieces, however, can move over a piece
of an opponent's color to capture that piece.

Pawns

Pawns have the most limited movement of any piece.

A pawn can only move forward a single square normally,
but if it is still in the starting position,
it can move two squares forward in a single turn.

The pawn is also the only piece that cannot
capture in the same way that it moves.

The pawn captures forward diagonally.

Knights

Knights have one of the strangest
movement patterns.

The knight can move in a L shape,
two squares in one direction
and another square at a right angle
to the starting direction.

Knights can also jump over pieces,
and cannot be boxed in.
